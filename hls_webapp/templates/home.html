We hear you. And now others can too.

There are currently 466 million people living with a hearing impairment. This application is meant to provide a glimpse into how the world sounds to a hearing impaired person.

Start Simulation

What

The Audemo simulator is a customizable tool for hearing impaired individuals to demonstrate to others what their auditory experience is like- what they can and cannot hear and how that varies between situations and environments.

How

  1. Starting with a mathematical model of the auditory system, users customize the simulator by entering their audiogram data and answering a few questions about speech intelligibility.

  2. The user uploads the sound file they wish to use for the simulation.

  3. The situation and environment parameters are set.

  4. The simulator produces an output sound that has been modified to represent how that sound would be perceived by the individual with impaired hearing.

Why

Communication is the foundation of all human relationships. To successfully communicate, there must be a “talker” and a “listener.” For those with impaired hearing, being a listener can be a frustrating and cognitively demanding activity. Listening is a complex process that many of us take for granted. If someone tells you they are hard of hearing, your first instinct may be to simply speak louder. However, this often results in more harm than good. Successful communication depends on a lot more than volume level. Many other factors play a significant role in the ability to understand what is being said, like background noise, distance between speakers, eye contact, visibility of the speaker’s mouth, and direction of sound. While the Audemo simulator may not be able to precisely simulate all of these factors, we hope it will give individuals with normal hearing a glimpse into the intricate auditory world of impaired hearing. We hope this glimpse will increase understanding and patience and can be used as the basis for conversations on how to improve communication.

Disclaimer

Simulating a disability is nearly impossible. Millions of Americans live day to day with all aspects of life affected by their disability. Simply stated- a brief activity can never replace a lifetime of experiences. ​ The disability experience is not designed to mimic these experiences but rather highlight how often elements of disability are easily overlooked in our world. A significant impact of a disability is often invisible… and a good point to remember is that many disabilities themselves are invisible. The challenge of a disability is not simply physical… it is social, environmental, and attitudinal. Faced with these challenges, individuals often adapt and develop coping skills, none of which are represented in a simulation. Pity, sympathy, judgement, and focusing on one obvious difficulty encountered is not encouraged. Instead, this simulation is intended to encourage patience, understanding, awareness, and conversation on the role we all play in fostering an inclusive environment.
